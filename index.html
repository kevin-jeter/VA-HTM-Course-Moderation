Moderator Guide

User Guide

Welcome to the HTM Moderator Guide. This guide is designed to provide links, resources, and instructions to help you effectively moderate all HTM Courses.

As a moderator, you will have access to various tools and resources to assist you in your role. Please refer to the sections below for detailed information on each lab and its associated resources.

Moderator Checklist

Basic Structure

A comprehensive checklist for moderators to ensure all aspects of the class are covered, including preparation, during-class activities, and post-class follow-up.

Day Before the Class

  • Email anything not yet sent to Co-Instructors and Moderators, the DAY BEFORE
  • Read ALL the email from your Co-Instructors and Moderators
  • Create a Group Address to text all Co-Instructors and Moderators during class, and TEST it the day before
  • Check your computer health (Any updates to do? Equipment working properly? Good wi-fi connection?)
  • Print relevant documents: Agenda, Contact List, Instructor Guide (or notes or PP), List of Break Out Rooms/Participants
  • Check and change any weak or dead battery, and make sure computer(s) is charging up

One Hour Before the Class

  • Set volumes on phones in the area and other devices to low or silent
  • Turn off Notifications on the computer
  • Remind others in your office or house that you will be starting a presentation
  • Check your workstation, Internet connection, and all monitors, headsets, and microphones
  • Refresh your beverage, your throat lozenges, your tissue, yourself
  • Open all the files you will need (PowerPoint, etc.)
  • Test any links or videos you plan to share
  • Line up beverages, throat lozenges, or whatever you might need
  • Ensure you have a way to take notes: blank document, paper and pen, or whatever will not interfere

45 to 30 Minutes Before Class

  • Login to Class, turn on Audio and Video, and check your appearance
  • Watch for people waiting in lobby and allow them in
  • Greet people as they join the session, and ask for volume checks
  • Check your lighting and adjust if needed
  • Smile: a smile on your face and a smile in your voice
  • Become your animated, enthusiastic, excited Instructor self, full of energy

At the Start of Class

  • Announce the hard stop Breaks and Lunch Breaks
  • Introduce all the Instructors and Moderators, and advise Participants about requesting Help
  • Review the Toolbar, Chat, Raising Hand, Mute
  • Set expectations for the class, (learning objectives, rules, and expectations of engagement, and methods to interact

Enhancing the Online Learning Experience

  • Check on Participant progress with frequent opportunities for interactions and make adjustments if you need to do so
  • Make occasional use of the “Teach by Asking” technique (Socratic method) to stimulate critical thinking
  • Use eye contact effectively! Remember to look at your camera
  • Follow the 5 X 5 Rule: Keep PowerPoints free of heavy descriptions; use bulleted lists, do not just read the PPTs
  • Make note of anything you promise to email after the session
  • Check on Breakout Groups to make sure students are in their respective groups and are working together. Follow up by letting them know when to return.
  • Provide at least two 15-minute breaks during the day and a 45-minute lunch. Breaks/time can be increased, but not decreased. Factor breaks into your agenda so they do not get cut when other activities take too long.

Ending the Class

  • Describe next steps, questions, and concerns, and contact information for last-minute questions
  • Remind students about the Course Evaluation; provide prior to or just after last break if necessary to ensure more participation
  • End graciously and on time. Participants have other time commitments to meet.
Tip: Follow this checklist meticulously to ensure a smooth and engaging class session.

    Quiz and Software Resources

    Wayground Navigation and Usage for Tests

    Wayground (formerly Quizizz) is an online platform for creating, hosting, and managing quizzes and tests. It is used in various HTM courses for assessments, practice tests, and interactive learning. Below are step-by-step instructions for moderators to navigate and use Wayground for creating and administering tests.

    Step 1: Accessing Wayground

    1. Navigate to the Wayground website: https://wayground.com/.
    2. We have a Kilda Group account. If you need the login information, please ask one of the team members

    Step 2: Hosting and Administering the Test

    1. From the Home dashboard, select "Library" on the left hand side.
    2. Search for the desired quiz/test using the search bar or select "Courses" to view the course collections.
    3. To choose the quiz, just click on "View Quiz" and then "Open Quiz"
    4. Next, just select "Start Live Session." Click through all the screens (Continue in top right and then "Start Now"). No settings need to be adjusted.
    5. To share the link, click the "copy" icon next to the top step and paste in the MS Teams chat. This link includes the game code for students to join. Remember to remind students to NOT enter their real names!
    6. After most students have joined, click the "Start" button. When most have finished (90-95%+), select the "End Session" in the top right.
    7. To review questions, don't back out all the way! You just need to click on "Questions" in the middle of the screen. Once doing so, you can select the question you want to review by clicking on it.
    8. The final note is to see the actual analytics per question. Just simply click on the question to see how students were answering it.

    Amazon Workspaces Moderation

    Every class that uses AWS has a few similarities. They are listed below.

    • Students will be given a username and password to log into their AWS Workspace. You can find this in the respective class Course Content on the VA Side
    • Students will NEVER need to download the Amazon Workspaces Client Application to access their workspace
    • Students Registration Codes and Passwords are NOT unique (all will be exactly the same)
    • Students Usernames are ALWAYS unique. Please refer to the Excel Spreadsheet in the VA Course Content for their respective Usernames
    • Most errors are user-based, not Amazon-based. Always start with having them screenshare to see what could be wrong.
    • Start by asking what username the student is using and what error messages they see, if any. It might also help to ask for a screenshot or for the student to share their screen. Then, find the corresponding error below. Follow the steps until the issue is resolved.

    As a moderator, your role is to help students troubleshoot any issues they have logging into or using their AWS Workspace during class. The most common issues and their solutions are listed below.

    AWS Troubleshooting Guide

    How to Reboot and How to Assign a New Workspace are at the top of this document. It is best practice to reassign a workspace when you reboot in the middle of a lab or when a student has multiple issues with a workspace. If you are ever unsure of what to do, assign a new workspace.

    You will need to be logged in to the AWS Management Console to check on the status of workspaces and reboot as needed. You will also need to have the AWS Login Information sheet for the course open to track assigned usernames.

    A normal workspace will look like this:

    How To Reboot

    1. Log in to Account ID: 566937488447
    2. Search for “WorkSpaces” in search bar
    3. Select first option
    4. Click “Workspaces”
    5. A full list of all created workspaces should look like this
    6. Select the workspace you need to reboot by clicking on the checkbox for that row. You can select multiple spaces at once, or click the checkbox in the column to select all visible on the page
    7. Once you select workspaces, options will be available along the top. Select “Reboot” from these option
    8. While rebooting, the status will look like this
    9. Let the student know they need to wait 5 minutes for the reboot to complete

    How to Assign a New Workspace

    When setting up AWS for a course, extra spaces will always be created as a backup. These spaces are listed at the bottom of the AWS Login Information sheet sent to students. They are usually unmarked and with lots of blank rows between them and the assigned workspaces to avoid students looking at them.

    1. Find an available backup space from the list
    2. Double check that it’s working properly by logging in and checking on its status in the management console
    3. Let the student know in the chat that you’re assigning them to this new workspace. (Ex. “Please switch to HTM50 for the remainder of the class”)
    4. In the AWS Login Information sheet, change the student’s username to the newly assigned space
    5. Move their original username to the bottom, in a separate column from the available spaces
    6. Troubleshoot as time permits

    The Username a Student Gives Doesn’t Match their Assigned Username

    1. Only one person can log into a workspace at one time. If the student is using a different username than what they were assigned, this will cause issues both for them and for the other student who is assigned to that space.
    2. Remind the student what their actual username is

    Invalid Registration Code

    1. Most likely there is some issue with the copy/paste.
    2. Ask student to type out registration code instead of pasting into AWS

    Invalid Username/Password

    1. Most likely there is some issue with the copy/paste.
    2. Ask student to type out username and password instead of pasting
    3. If error persists, check they have the correct credentials

    Some students will also attempt to login to the Amazon Workspace App within their workspace. If their screen looks like this screenshot, ask them to close the internal workspace window.

    “My Workspace is taking too long to log in”

    1. Check the health of their workspace on. If the status is “Unhealthy”, reboot the workspace
    2. Let the student know they need to wait 5 minutes for the reboot to complete
    3. Ask the student to close out of AWS, clear the cache and cookies on their browser, and try again
    4. If problem persists, assign a new workspace

    ERROR_TYPE_CONNECTION

    This error can indicate that someone else has logged into the workspace, or that there was some connection issue while logging in, like the connection timed out. Ask the student to close or refresh their browser then try again

    If the error comes back:

    1. Make sure the student isn’t logging in to the internal Amazon Workspace App within their workspace..
    2. Check the health of their workspace on If the status is “Unhealthy”, reboot the workspace and let the student know they need to wait 5 minutes for the reboot to complete
    3. If the status is “Available”, assign a new workspace

    AWS is a black screen

    Immediately reboot the workspace. If error persists, assign a new workspace.

    “The Workspace is Very Slow / I can’t click or type / The screen is frozen”

    1. This might be caused by connectivity issues on the student’s side.
    2. If at VA Facility, ensure they are NOT on the guest network & recommend obtaining a secondary device. If at home network, ensure they are NOT on VPN & recommend closing all tabs/other windows.
    3. Ask student to refresh their browser and clear cache/cookies
    4. Reboot the workspace
    5. If issue persists, assign a new workspace

    It Looks Weird / MISC Errors

    “It keeps kicking me out”. This is vague and could be caused by several things like slow connection, someone else logging into the workspace, etc. Ask the student to share screen to troubleshoot further

    ERR_SIG_TIMEOUT is another connection error. If at VA Facility, ensure they are NOT on the guest network & recommend obtaining a secondary device. If at home network, ensure they are NOT on VPN & recommend closing all tabs/other windows.
